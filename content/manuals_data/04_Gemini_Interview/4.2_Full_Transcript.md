## Session Mechanics: The "Signal" vs. The "Noise"

**Interviewer:** Why is the "quick-reaction" method important for capturing raw data?

**Agent 'X':** The quick reaction response procedure allowed PK-Agents to give gut reaction 1st intuition answers.

**Interviewer:** That's exactly right. That "gut reaction" or "1st intuition" is precisely the "raw perception" or "signal" the trainers were trying to capture. The core idea was to get the viewer to state that initial impression *before* their slower, conscious, analytical brain had time to jump in and label it.

### Raw Perception (The "Signal")

This is the direct, unprocessed sensory data the viewer receives. The GRILL FLAME manual gives examples:
- "Land surrounded by water"
- "Cold sensation"

### Analysis (The "Noise" or "Overlay")

This is the brain's automatic, often incorrect *interpretation* of the raw data.
- **Raw Perception:** "I sense a large, rectangular, man-made body of water."
- **Analysis/Overlay:** "It's a swimming pool."

The GRILL FLAME manual states that **"descriptive elements are generally of higher reliability than judgements or labels."** The entire training program was designed to solve this one problem: to separate the signal from the noise.

## Training Methods Progression (SUN STREAK)

The SUN STREAK manual outlines three specific "classes" of training—A, B, and C—designed to develop the viewer's skills.

### 1. Class C Training (Novice Phase)
- **Goal:** Learn the difference between genuine perceptions and "imaginative overlay."
- **Feedback:** Immediate, positive feedback only ("Correct," "Near," "Site"). Silence for wrong answers.

### 2. Class B Training (Intermediate Phase)
- **Goal:** Internalize awareness and build confidence.
- **Feedback:** Withdrawn. Interviewer knows the target but gives **no direct feedback** during the session.

### 3. Class A Training (Double-Blind Phase)
- **Goal:** Operational readiness; relying *only* on RV ability.
- **Procedure:** A **"double-blind"** session where *neither* the trainee *nor* the interviewer has any information about the target. This controls for cueing and "telepathically" picking up the answer from the interviewer's mind.
