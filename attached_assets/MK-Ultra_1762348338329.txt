**MEMORANDUM FOR: Director of Central Intelligence**
**SUBJECT: Report of Inspection of MKULTRA**
**26 July 1963**

1.  [cite_start]In connection with our survey of Technical Services Division, DD/P, it was deemed advisable to prepare the report of the MKULTRA program in one copy only, in view of its unusual sensitivity. [cite: 535]

2.  [cite_start]This report is forwarded herewith. [cite: 536]

3.  [cite_start]The MKULTRA activity is concerned with the research and development of chemical, biological, and radiological materials capable of employment in clandestine operations to control human behavior. [cite: 537-539] [cite_start]The end products of such research are subject to very strict controls including a requirement for the personal approval of the Deputy Director/Plans for any operational use made of these end products. [cite: 539]

4.  [cite_start]The cryptonym MKULTRA encompasses the R&D phase and a second cryptonym MKDELTA denotes the DD/P system for control of the operational employment of such materials. [cite: 540] [cite_start]...The administration and control of this latter activity were found to be generally satisfactory and are discussed in greater detail in the main body of the report on TSD. [cite: 541-542]

5.  [cite_start]MKULTRA was authorized by the then Director of Central Intelligence, Mr. Allen W. Dulles, in 1953. [cite: 543] [cite_start]The TSD was assigned responsibility thereby to employ a portion of its R&D budget... under purely internal and compartmented controls... [cite: 543] [cite_start]Normal procedures for project approval, funding, and accounting were waived. [cite: 544] [cite_start]However, special arrangements for audit of expenditures have been evolved in subsequent years. [cite: 545]

6.  [cite_start]The scope of MKULTRA is comprehensive and ranges from the search for and procurement of botanical and chemical substances, through programs for their analysis in scientific laboratories, to progressive testing for effect on animals and human beings. [cite: 557] [cite_start]The testing on individuals begins under laboratory conditions employing every safeguard and progresses gradually to more and more realistic operational simulations. [cite: 558] [cite_start]The program requires and obtains the services of a number of highly specialized authorities in many fields of the natural sciences. [cite: 559]

7.  [cite_start]The concepts involved in manipulating human behavior are found by many people both within and outside the Agency to be distasteful and unethical. [cite: 560] [cite_start]There is considerable evidence that opposition intelligence services are active and highly proficient in this field. [cite: 561] [cite_start]The experience of TSD to date indicates that both the research and the employment of the materials are expensive and often unpredictable in results. [cite: 562] [cite_start]Nevertheless, there have been major accomplishments both in research and operational employment. [cite: 563]

8.  [cite_start]The principal conclusions of the inspection are that the structure and operational controls over this activity need strengthening; [cite: 564] [cite_start]improvements are needed in the administration of the research projects; [cite: 565] [cite_start]and some of the testing of substances under simulated operational conditions was judged to involve excessive risk to the Agency. [cite: 566]

9.  [cite_start]Attached for the signature of the Deputy Director of Central Intelligence is a memorandum transmitting the report to the Deputy Director/Plans requesting a summary of action taken or comments on the recommendations contained therein. [cite: 567]

**J. Earman**
[cite_start]**Inspector General** [cite: 570]

### REPORT OF INSPECTION OF MKULTRA/TSD

#### I. Introduction

1.  [cite_start]Technical Services Division (TSD), (then Technical Support Staff), received authorization from the then Director of Central Intelligence, Mr. Allen W. Dulles, on 3 April 1953 to develop and maintain continuing operational capabilities in the fields of a) [REDACTED] and b) chemical and biological materials capable of producing human behavioral and physiological changes, (see Tab A). [cite: 578, 584] [cite_start]The cryptonym MKULTRA was assigned to encompass TSD's research, development and equipment activities in these two fields. [cite: 585] [cite_start]The cryptonym MKDELTA had already been assigned... as the indicator covering DD/P policy and procedure for the use of biochemicals in clandestine operations. [cite: 586]

2.  [cite_start]The MKULTRA charter provides only a brief presentation of the rationale of the authorized activities. [cite: 587] [cite_start]The sensitive aspects of the program as it has evolved over the ensuing ten years are the following: [cite: 588]
    * a. [cite_start]Research in the manipulation of human behavior is considered by many authorities in medicine and related fields to be professionally unethical, therefore the reputations of professional participants in the MKULTRA program are on occasion in jeopardy. [cite: 589-601]
    * b. [cite_start]Some MKULTRA activities raise questions of legality implicit in the original charter. [cite: 602-603]
    * c. [cite_start]A final phase of the testing of MKULTRA products places the rights and interests of U. S. citizens in jeopardy. [cite: 604]
    * d. [cite_start]Public disclosure of some aspects of MKULTRA activity could induce serious adverse reaction in U. S. public opinion, as well as stimulate offensive and defensive action in this field on the part of foreign intelligence services. [cite: 605]

#### II. Modus Operandi

6.  [cite_start]The research and development of materials capable of producing behavioral or physiological change in humans is now performed within a highly elaborated and stabilized MKULTRA structure. [cite: 677] [cite_start]The search for new materials; e.g., psilocybin from Mexican mushrooms, or a fungi occurring in agricultural crops, is conducted through standing arrangements with specialists in universities, pharmaceutical houses, hospitals, state and federal institutions, and private research organizations... [cite: 678] [cite_start]Annual grants of funds are made under ostensible research foundation auspices to the specialists... [cite: 679] [cite_start]This approach conceals from the institution the interest of CIA and permits the recipient to proceed with his investigation, publish his findings (excluding intelligence implications), and account for his expenditures in a manner normal to his institution. [cite: 680] [cite_start]...Key individuals must qualify for top secret clearance and are made witting of Agency sponsorship. [cite: 690] [cite_start]As a rule each specialist is managed unilaterally and is not witting of Agency support of parallel MKULTRA research in his field. [cite: 691] [cite_start]The system in effect "buys a piece" of the specialist in order to enlist his aid in pursuing the intelligence implications of his research. [cite: 692]

7.  [cite_start]The funding of sensitive MKULTRA projects by sterile grants in aid... disclosed one of the principal controversial aspects of this program. [cite: 695] [cite_start]The original charter... assumed that the sensitivity... would be sufficient to justify both a) special protection for the researcher; and b) compartmentation of MKULTRA knowledge within the Agency. [cite: 696-698] [cite_start]On this basis the inherent safeguards of DD/S procedures were waived... [cite: 698]

8.  [cite_start]The next phase of the MKULTRA program involves physicians, toxicologists, and other specialists in mental, narcotics, and general hospitals and in prisons, who are provided the products and findings of the basic research projects and proceed with intensive testing on human subjects. [cite: 709] [cite_start]...Materials and procedures with intelligence potential may be identified through this relationship. [cite: 711] [cite_start]The testing programs are conducted under accepted scientific procedures including the use of control populations, the employment of placebos, and the detailed observation, measurement, recording, analysis, and publication of findings. [cite: 717] [cite_start]Where health permits, test subjects are voluntary participants in the program. [cite: 718]

9.  [cite_start]A current development in the testing of new products is the tightening of controls over dosages and procedures by the U. S. Food and Drug Administration. [cite: 719] [cite_start]...interviews with the TSD officers concerned indicated that the new rules are affecting procedures and causing controversy in research hospitals and pharmaceutical houses. [cite: 721]

10. [cite_start]The final phase of testing of MKULTRA materials involves their application to unwitting subjects in normal life settings. [cite: 723] [cite_start]...the capabilities of MKULTRA substances to produce disabling or discrediting effects or to increase the effectiveness of interrogation of hostile subjects cannot be established solely through testing on volunteer populations. 11. [cite_start]TSD, therefore, entered into an informal arrangement with certain cleared and witting individuals in the Bureau of Narcotics in 1955 which provided for the release of MKULTRA materials for such testing as those individuals deemed desirable and feasible.
